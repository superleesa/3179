Japan's Deflation

Japan has been grappling with deflation for many years. Deflation is a persistent decrease in the general price level of goods and services, and it can have various economic consequences, including a decrease in demand and wages, and increase in unemployement.

What is current Japan's deflation level?

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is often used as an economic indicator for inflation/deflation level, which specifically measures the average change in prices paid by consumers for a basket of goods and services over time. The below chart shows the CPI for G7 countries in various sectors, such as food, energy, transporation, clothing and communication. It clealy indicates a Japan's deflating trend, where CPI values are not increasing and sometimes decreasing, when other countries sustain about 2 CPI units increase per year.

What's causing and not causing the deflation?

Fact1: Minimum wage is increasing

Japan's government constantly elevates minimum wages, optimized prefecture-wise. Slide the slider to see the increasing minimum wage along the timeline.

Fact2: Work producitivity is the lowest among G7 countries

Economists argue that Japan's deflation is caused by its inability to be productive and creative, leading to non-increasing wages and product price levels.

Fact3: Average Wage is NOT increasing even slighly compared to other countries

The increasing minimum wage doesn't direcly correlate to Japan's average wage. When average wage is low, people do not have adequate purchasing power, necessiating companies to decrease porducts/services price level. The below bump chart shows the dropping ranking, from 5th to 20th, in Japan's average wage compared to OECD countries, from 2000 to 2022.
