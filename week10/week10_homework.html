Japan: Deflation

Japan has been grappling with deflation for many years. Deflation is a persistent decrease in the general price level of goods and services, and it can have various economic consequences. However, it seems that Japan currently is in stagflation, a even worse situation, where the price level gets higher without increasing wages.

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is often used as an economic indicator for inflation/deflation level, which specifically measures the average change in prices paid by consumers for a basket of goods and services over time.
